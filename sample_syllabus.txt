Business Analytics Course Syllabus

Course Title: Introduction to Business Analytics
Instructor: Dr. Jane Smith
Semester: Spring 2026

Course Description:
This course provides an introduction to business analytics, covering data-driven decision making, statistical analysis, and predictive modeling techniques used in modern business environments.

Learning Outcomes:
By the end of this course, students will be able to:

1. Analyze business data using statistical methods to identify patterns and trends
2. Apply data visualization techniques to communicate insights effectively to stakeholders
3. Evaluate the quality and reliability of data sources for business decision-making
4. Design and implement predictive models to forecast business outcomes
5. Interpret analytical results and translate them into actionable business recommendations
6. Utilize industry-standard analytics software and tools for data analysis
7. Recognize ethical considerations in data collection, analysis, and reporting

Course Objectives:
- Develop critical thinking skills for data-driven problem solving
- Understand the role of analytics in strategic business planning
- Build proficiency in statistical analysis and data interpretation
- Create professional analytical reports and presentations

Assessment Methods:

1. Midterm Examination (25%)
   - Covers statistical analysis and data visualization concepts
   - Format: Multiple choice, short answer, and practical problems
   
2. Final Project (30%)
   - Team-based analytics project using real business data
   - Includes data analysis, modeling, and presentation components
   - Deliverables: Written report and oral presentation

3. Weekly Assignments (25%)
   - Individual homework assignments applying course concepts
   - Mix of theoretical and practical problems
   
4. Case Study Analysis (15%)
   - Two case studies analyzing real-world business analytics applications
   - Individual written submissions

5. Class Participation (5%)
   - Active engagement in class discussions
   - Contribution to group activities

Required Materials:
- Textbook: Business Analytics: Data Analysis & Decision Making (7th Edition)
- Software: Excel, Tableau, R or Python (student versions available)

Course Schedule:
Week 1-2: Introduction to Business Analytics
Week 3-4: Data Collection and Preparation
Week 5-6: Descriptive Statistics and Visualization
Week 7-8: Probability and Statistical Inference
Week 9-10: Regression Analysis and Forecasting
Week 11-12: Predictive Modeling Techniques
Week 13-14: Advanced Analytics Applications
Week 15: Final Project Presentations
